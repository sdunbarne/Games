# Games
Four board games, of increasing size, modeled as Markov chains.
1.      *Tenzi* is a simple dice game for multiple players.  The
        gameplay of Tenzi for a single player can be modeled with a
        Markov chain with \( 10 \) transient states and \( 1 \)
        absorbing state.  Markov chain methods give the distribution of
        the number of rolls necessary to complete the game, along with
        the mean and variance.
2.      The childrens' game *Chutes and Ladders* is a \( 100 \)
        state Markov chain with a final absorbing state and Markov chain
        methods give the expected playing time.
3.      *Count Your Chickens!* is a cooperative counting game aimed
        at preschoolers.  Absorbing Markov chains give the probability
        of winning a game of *Count Your Chickens!* and the
        expected number of chicks at the end of the game.  The main
        interest is classifying and counting the states, leading to a \(
        165 \times 165 \) transition probability matrix.
 4      *Risk* is a board game of battles between two players, and
        each battle consists of one or more rounds of combat between \(
        A \) attacking armies and \( D \) defending armies.  The goal is
        to develop the probability of winning the battle as a function
        of \( A \) and \( D \) using a Markov chain with \( AD + A + D \)
        states.
